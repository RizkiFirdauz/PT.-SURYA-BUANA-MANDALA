    WORK METHOD

    PRE-HEATING

    it is the application of the heat to the base metal prior to the welding or cutting operations. This does not change the properties of steel, but is recommended in a certain circumstance to repel the moistureand ensure, even to expand and eliminate the unexpected stress. The concurrent heating durring welding (also called heating) inhibits the cooling in order to prevent the formulation of down bead cracking.

    POST WELD HEAT TREATMENT

    it is heating the steel to a relatively high temperature and maintaining the temperature for a certain period. it is to reduce the internal pressure or residual stress, caused by welding, heardening, or with cold working. it also has a beneficial effect on ductility, elasticity and strength of metal. This process involves in heating the steel to a temperature below the transformation point of material.

    ANNEALING

    it is softening metal to make it softer, generally. This process involves heating and cooling in a slow control to lead the softness and ductility. in addition, the process of removing gas, reducing stress and improving the mechanical properties of steel. This is genneraly done at the temperature above the recrystallized point material.

    NORMALIZING

    it is much more different than the ANNEALING, but the cooling process takes faster. this leads to increase the strength but to decrease the ductility in metals. it also produces higher yield strength with the greater tensile strength, and in most cases, take higher impact resistance. This process involves heating the steel just above the upper critical limit.
